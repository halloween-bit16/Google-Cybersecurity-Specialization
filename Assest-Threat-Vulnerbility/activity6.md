# Attack Surfaces & Attack Vectors – Home Environment Self-Reflection

---

## Question 1 – Attack Surfaces of a Home

A home has both physical and digital attack surfaces. Physical surfaces include entry points like doors, windows, and garage access, while digital surfaces encompass Wi-Fi networks, smart devices, computers, and connected home systems like thermostats or security cameras. Many of these surfaces are continuously exposed to risk, especially internet-connected devices that remain active around the clock.

---

## Question 2 – Attack Vectors and Defenses

The attack vectors for a home include unlocked or weak physical entry points, unsecured Wi-Fi networks, outdated device firmware, weak or reused passwords, and phishing attempts targeting household members. An attacker could exploit an unpatched smart doorbell or guess a default router password to gain access to the entire home network. To defend against these vectors, I can apply strategies like enabling WPA3 encryption on the router, using multi-factor authentication on accounts, regularly updating firmware, and segmenting IoT devices onto a separate guest network. Physical defenses such as deadbolt locks, security cameras, and motion-sensor lighting further reduce risk by eliminating easy access points.
